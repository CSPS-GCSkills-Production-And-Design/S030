ICS in Canada

2002 - Canadian Interagency Forest Fire Centre (CIFFC) and member Agencies

Complete curriculum for forest fires

Recognized all-hazards application

In 2002 the Canadian Interagency Forest Fire Centre, or CIFFC, and member agencies adopted the Incident Command System. Several ICS documents and a complete training curriculum were developed and adopted by forest fire agencies across the country. The Canadian Interagency Forest Fire Centre ICS curriculum focused on the organizational structure and processes for managing wildland fires but was adopted as the Canadian standard ICS system by a number of provinces and local authorities. ICS Canada has now expanded well beyond wildland fire response to provide a pan-Canadian ICS command and control structure that will enable organizations to respond to any incident, regardless of cause, size, location or complexity. Across Canada, other emergency response organizations and agencies, including a growing number of federal departments have adopted ICS.
