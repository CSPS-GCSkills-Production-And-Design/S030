Transfer of Command

One of the main features of ICS is the ability to transfer command with minimum disruption.

Command may change to meet the needs of the incident when incidents:

  • Expand or contract

  • Change in jurisdiction or discipline

  • Become more or less complex command may change to meet the needs of the incident.

As incidents grow, the Incident Commander may delegate authority for performance of certain activities to the Command Staff and General Staff. The Incident Commander will add positions only as needed.


 

Transfer of Command requires:

  • A command briefing for the incoming Incident Commander

  • Notification to all personnel that a change in command is taking place

 

Transfer of command may take place when:

  • A senior person arrives and elects or has been designated by a higher authority to assume the position of Incident Commander. This is often associated with a growing incident.

  • Transfer of command can also take place in reverse, for example, transferring command to a less senior or less qualified person in an incident which is under control or moving toward demobilization.

  • New operational periods begin

  • Lower ranking, more qualified person is best selection because of the unique circumstances associated with the incident.

Transfer of Command briefing includes:

  • Situation status

  • Objectives and priorities

  • Current organization

  • Resource assignments

  • Resources en route and/or ordered

  • Facilities established

  • Communications Plan

  • Prognosis, concerns, related issues

Note : The Incident Briefing Form (ICS Form 201), is a useful tool used by the IC to track incident information and notes during the initial stages of an incident. It is also the form used to brief other Incident Commanders if a transfer of command is required.
