Incident Commander (IC)

 

As illustrated on the chart, the ICS organization may be expanded to include Command Staff positions, which can include an Information Officer, Liaison Officer, and Safety Officer, as well as General Staff positions which include an Operations Section Chief, Planning Section Chief, Logistics Section Chief, and Finance/Administration Section Chief. This unit focuses on the Command function which includes the Incident Commander position as well as the Command Staff positions.

 

The Incident Commander performs all major ICS command and general staff responsibilities unless these functions are delegated and assigned. The Incident Commander can delegate authority for these staff positions to others as the complexity of the incident or event dictates.

Not all Command and General Staff positions are filled on each incident. However, if a position isn’t filled, then the Incident Commander assumes that ICS management function.

 

All incident responses begin by establishing command. Usually the person in charge of the first arriving units at the scene of an incident assumes the Incident Commander role. That person will remain in charge until formally relieved, or until transfer of command is accomplished.

Upon arriving at an incident, a higher ranking person will either:

  • assume command

  • maintain command

  • transfer command

Maintaining Command is a good practice to develop junior officers.

In some situations or agencies, a lower ranking but more qualified person may be designated as the Incident Commander.

Question :  Why is it critical to establish command from the beginning of the incident operations?

ANSwer


  • Lack of command becomes a safety hazard for responders.

  • Size up and decision making is impossible without a command structure.

  • It is difficult to expand a disorganized organization if the incident escalates.

Incident Commander’s Role

  • Provides overall leadership for incident response or management of an event.

  • Sets incident objectives, strategies and priorities using the standard Priorities of Life Safety, Incident Stabilization and Property and Environmental protection.

  • Delegates authority to others. The Incident Commander must be fully briefed and should have a written delegation of authority from the agency of jurisdiction.

  • Takes general direction from Agency Executive/Administrator or official.

Note : The Incident Commander is the only position that is always staffed in ICS applications.

The Incident Commander has overall responsibility for managing the incident by establishing objectives, planning strategies, and implementing tactics. On small incidents and events, one person, the Incident Commander, may accomplish all management functions.

In addition to having overall responsibility for managing the entire incident, the Incident Commander is specifically responsible for:

  • Ensuring incident safety

  • Providing information services (internal/external)

  • Establishing and maintaining liaison with other agencies

  • Assessing need for staff

  • Directing staff to develop the Incident Action Plan

  • Establishing incident objectives

  • All activities and functions until delegated
