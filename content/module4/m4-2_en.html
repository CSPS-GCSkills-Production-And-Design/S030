Five Major Management Functions

Every incident or event requires that certain management functions be performed.

There are five of these major management functions, and it is these five functions that are the foundation upon which the ICS organization develops, rather than the administrative structure of any single agency or jurisdiction.

These functions apply whether you are handling a routine response, organizing for a major non-emergency event, or managing a response to a major incident or emergency.

Note

As outlined in Unit 3, command can be carried out in two ways; Single Command or Unified Command.

For this unit we will focus on Single Command, as it is the most common application.

The five major management functions are:

    

Incident Command

Sets the incident objectives, strategies, and priorities and has overall responsibility for the incident.

Operations Section

Conducts operations to reach the incident objectives. Establishes the strategies and tactics and directs all operational resources.

Planning Section

Supports the incident action planning process by tracking resources, collecting/analyzing information, and maintaining documentation.

Logistics Section

Provides resources and needed services to support the achievement of the incident objectives.

Finance/Administration Section

Monitors costs related to the incident. Provides accounting, procurement, time recording, and cost analyses.
