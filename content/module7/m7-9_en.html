Incident Record Keeping

 

 

All incidents require some form of record keeping. Requirements will vary depending upon the agencies involved and the kind, size and complexity of the incident. Detailed information on how to use ICS forms will be covered in further ICS training; however there are several general considerations relating to incident record keeping.

 

Keeping Accurate Records

  • Print or type all entries and include your name

  • Use month/day/year format

  • Use 24-hour (local) time

  • Enter date and time on all forms and records

  • Fill in all blanks, use N/A as appropriate

  • Section Chiefs and above may assign a record keeper, or scribe, to capture information

The record keeper should not report directly to the particular Section Chief they are scribing for, due to concerns around span of control and potential reassignment to unrelated tasks.

 

Supervisory Responsibilities

  • Maintain a daily Unit Log (ICS Form 214), which indicates the names of personnel assigned

  • Provide briefings to:

    • Subordinates

    • Adjacent forces

    • Replacement personnel
