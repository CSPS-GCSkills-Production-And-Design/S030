Initial Incident Briefing

After check-in, you should locate your incident point of contact and obtain an initial incident briefing. The information received in the briefing will be important for your own planning and for passing on accurate and up to date information to any subordinate staff you may have.

Initial Incident Briefing should include:

  • Current situation assessment and objectives

  • Specific job responsibilities

  • Location of work area

  • Procedures for obtaining additional resources

  • Safety hazards and required safety procedures or personal protective equipment

  • Identify operational period work shifts

After receiving the initial briefing and activating your assignment, it is important to give a similar briefing to any personnel that are assigned to you.
