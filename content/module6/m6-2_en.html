Incident Command Post

This is the map symbol of the Incident Command Post.

Every incident must have some form of an Incident Command Post (ICP).

  • One per incident even if it is a multijurisdictional or multi agency incident operating under a single or unified command

  • Location at which the primary command functions are performed. The planning function is also normally done at the ICP. The initial location for the ICP should consider the nature of the incident, whether it is growing or moving, and whether the ICP will be suitable in size and safe for the expected duration of the incident. It is usually, but not necessarily, located with the Base which will be described shortly.

  • Positioned outside of present and potential hazard zone but close enough to the incident to maintain command

  • May co-locate: Vehicle, trailer, tent, building

  • May change locations

Question: 

What additional considerations are important when deciding where to locate the ICP?

Answer

The Incident Command Post should always be upwind from any hazardous materials releases, easily accessible to responders, and likely to have sustained communications systems and power.

On longer term incidents it is desirable to provide an ICP facility which will provide adequate lighting and/or protection from the weather. The ICP should be large enough to provide adequate working room for assigned personnel. On expanding incidents, an ICP may move if an improved location is required to facilitate command operations. The ICP will be designated by the name of the incident (for example, Ambassador Bridge ICP).
