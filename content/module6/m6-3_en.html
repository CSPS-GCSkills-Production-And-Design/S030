Staging Area

This is the map symbol of the Staging Area.

Staging Area:

  • Temporary location at an incident where available personnel and/or equipment are kept while waiting for tactical assignments

  • May have several

  • May be located in the same immediate area as the ICP, Bases, Camps, Helibases, or Helispots, but it should have its own name that describes the general location, such as Webster Park Staging Area.

  • Staging Area Manager is required, who reports to the Operations Section Chief. A Staging Area Manager is always assigned

  • Resources available for deployment. Therefore, resources that are mechanically out of service, and personnel resting or sleeping would not be in the Staging Area.

  • May be designated for specific kinds of resources such as Ambulances, Bull Dozers, etc.

  • May include fueling and sanitation

Staging Area Characteristics:

  • Close to tactical assignments

  • Away from hazard effects

  • Relocated if necessary

  • Different incoming and outgoing access

  • Large enough; room for growth

  • Clearly marked

  • Minimize environmental damage

Staging Area Benefits

  • Safe locations for immediately available resources

  • Allows for greater accountability by having available personnel and resources together in one location

  • Prevents freelancing

  • Minimizes excessive communications of resources calling for assignments

  • Resources managementcontrols and assists the check-in of personnel who arrive at the incident via privately owned vehicles or other private means

  • Properly plan for resource use, and to provide for contingencies

Note : Designating potential Staging Area locations and planning layouts in advance can be a part of an effective emergency planning process.
