Finance/Administration Section


The Finance/Administration Section Chief may not be activated on all incidents. As with all other sections, the Incident Commander will determine if there is a need for a Finance/Administration Section for the incident, and if yes, will designate an individual to fill the position of Finance/Administration Section Chief. If no Finance/Administration Section is established, the Incident Commander will retain responsibility for all finance-related activities until either Finance/Administration units or the section has been activated.

Finance/Administration Section

  • Responsible for financial and cost analysis

  • Overseeing contract negotiations

  • Tracks personnel and equipment time

  • Processes claims for accidents and injuries

  • Works with Logistics to ensure resources are procured


This Section is responsible for:

  • Contract negotiation and administration;

  • Timekeeping, for both personnel and equipment;

  • Cost monitoring and analysis; and

  • Compensation for any injury or damage to property.

 

As the diagram shows, the Finance/Administration Section may staff up to four units. Not all units may be required; they will be established based on need. The four units which may be included are:

  • A Time Unit

  • A Procurement Unit

  • A Compensation/Claims Unit, and

  • A Cost Unit

Time Unit

  • Ensures that all personnel time on an incident or event is recorded

  • Check-in information is sent to this Unit in order to verify payroll.

Procurement Unit

  • Vendor contracts

  • Leases

  • Fiscal agreements

  • It is the responsibility of the Procurement Unit to oversee the recording of time for all equipment assigned to an incident, NOT the Time Unit.

Compensation/Claims Unit

  • Compensation for injury

  • Claims-related activities

This Unit combines two important functions:

  • First, Compensation is responsible for seeing that all documentation related to workers compensation is correctly completed. Also, Compensation maintains files of injuries and/or illnesses associated with the incident.

  • Secondly, Claims handles investigation of all claims involving damaged property associated with or involved in the incident. For example, if a farmer were to complain that responders tore down fencing to access his property allowing cows to get out and be lost, the initial investigation into this complaint would be conducted by this Unit.

Cost Unit

  • Collects all cost data

  • Performs cost effectiveness analyses

  • Provides cost estimates

  • Makes cost saving recommendations
