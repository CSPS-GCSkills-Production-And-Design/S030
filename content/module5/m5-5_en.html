ICS Supervisory Titles


As supervisory layers are added to the organizational structure, individuals with primary responsibility positions have distinct titles. Using specific ICS position titles serves three important purposes.

ICS Supervisory Titles

  • Provide a common standard for all users. For example, if one agency uses the title Branch Chief, another Branch manager, etc., this lack of consistency can cause confusion at the incident.

  • Allow for filling ICS positions with the most qualified individuals rather than by seniority.

  • Useful when requesting qualified personnel. For example, in deploying personnel It is important to know if the positions needed are Unit Leaders, Supervisors, etc.

As you can see in the table, according to the ICS position titles, the Incident Command function is filled by an Incident Commander. The Command Staff positions are Officers, the heads of General staff functions are referred to as Chiefs, Branches are lead by Directors, Divisions and Groups are led by Supervisors and Units, Strike Teams and Task Forces are lead by Leaders.
