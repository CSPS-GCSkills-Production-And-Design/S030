Maintaining Span of Control

   

 

Before we begin looking at each of the General Staff functions it is useful to remember the importance of span of control. Please click on each of the three questions below.

Ineffective and Possibly Dangerous

Effective Span of Control

Question 1

It is very common in early stages of an incident to exceed the maximum span of control, and this must be watched for closely. Why is it ineffective, and possibly dangerous, to exceed the maximum span of control?


Answer:

It is ineffective, and possibly dangerous, to exceed the maximum span of control because there is a limit to the number of individuals or resources that one supervisor can safely and effectively manage. Maintaining an effective span of control is particularly important on incidents where safety and accountability are a top priority.

Question 2

What is an acceptable span of control?


Answer:

An acceptable span of control is 3 to 7 resources reporting to a supervisor.

Question 3

What is the optimum span of control?


Answer:

The optimum span of control is 5.
