Planning Section

 

The second potential section that may be activated is the Planning Section. The Incident Commander will determine if there is a need for a Planning Section, and if yes, will designate a Planning Section Chief. If a Planning Section is not established, the Incident Commander will perform all of the planning functions.

Planning Section Chief

  • Gathers, analyses and disseminates information and intelligence

  • Manages planning process

  • Compiles Incident Action Plan

  • Manages Technical Specialists

  • May have a deputy

Planning Section

  • Maintains resource status

  • Maintains and displays situation status

  • Prepares the Incident Action Plan

  • Develops alternative strategies

  • Provides documentation services

  • Prepares the Demobilization Plan

  • Primary location for Technical Specialists

One of the most important functions of the Planning Section is to look beyond the current and next operational periods, and anticipate potential problems or events.

    

Resources Unit

  • Conducts / coordinates check-in activities

  • Maintains resource status

  • Significant role in preparing written Incident Action Plan

Situation Unit

  • Collects and analyzes information on current situation

  • Prepares situation displays and situation summaries

  • Develops maps and projections

  • It can include GIS specialists for mapping, etc.

Documentation Unit

  • Prepares photocopies of Incident Action Plan

  • Maintains and archives all incident-related documentation

  • Provides duplication services

Demobilization Unit

On large, complex incidents, the Demobilization Unit assists in ensuring that resources are released from the incident in an orderly, safe, and cost effective manner when they are no longer required at the incident.

  • Demobilization policy and procedures

  • Release priorities

  • Release procedures

Technical Specialists

Technical specialists are advisors with special skills required at the incident. Technical specialists will initially report to the Planning Section, work within that section, or be reassigned to another part of the organization. 

Technical specialists can be in any discipline required, such as aviation, environment, hazardous materials, and many others.

  • Provide specialized expertise

  • May be assigned to work in the Planning Section or in other Sections

Note : The Resources Unit does NOT acquire resources - that is the role of the Logistics Section- it tracks resources.
