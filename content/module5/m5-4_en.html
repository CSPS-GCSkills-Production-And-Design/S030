Expanding Operations

 

As incidents expand, in order to maintain a manageable span of control under the Incident Commander and General Staff Functions there may be the need to add supervisory layers to the organizational structure.

As the graphic shows, the Incident Commander may need to add Sections, such as an Operations Section. In addition:

  • The Operations Section may need to add Branches.

  • If the incident expands further, the Operations Section may divide the Branches into Divisions and/or Groups.

  • For even larger incidents, the Groups and or Divisions within the Operations Section may be divided into Task Forces and Strike Teams or Single Resources.

  • The other Sections may be subdivided into Units.

This unit will explain these components of the ICS organizational structure in depth.
