Operations Section: Responsibilities

 

One of the General Staff functions is the Operations Section Chief. Until Operations is established as a separate Section, the Incident Commander has direct control of tactical resources. The Incident Commander will determine the need for a separate Operations Section at an incident or event. When the Incident Commander activates an Operations Section he or she will assign an individual as the Operations Section Chief.

Operations Section Chief

  • Develops and implements strategies and tactics to accomplish the incident objectives set by the Incident Commander

  • Normally the person with the greatest technical and tactical expertise in dealing with the problem at hand

  • Organizes, assigns and supervises tactical resources field resources

  • Supervises:

    • Air Operations

    • Resources in a Staging Area

    • Branches, Divisions, Groups

The Operations Section is typically one of the first sections to be assigned to the incident as it directs and coordinates all incident tactical operations. The Operations Section also typically has the most incident resources.

   

Operations at an incident or event can be set up in a variety of ways depending upon the kind of incident, the agencies involved and the objectives and strategies.

The Operations Section will expand or contract based upon the existing and projected needs of the incident.

Unlike the other sections, this section develops from the bottom up starting with single resources that can be organized into task forces and/or strike teams which can be assigned to Divisions and/or Groups. If necessary, Branches can also be implemented to maintain proper span of control. Also, the Operations Section may have Staging Areas and, in some cases, an air organization. Often the most hazardous activities are carried out in the Operations Section, so it is necessary to carefully monitor the number of resources that report to any one supervisor.

Operations Section

  • Directs all tactical operations

  • Typically first element established

  • Expands from bottom up

  • Normally has most resources

  • May have Staging Areas
