Logistics Section


Logistics Section

  • Provide services and support to meet all incident or event needs

The Incident Commander will determine if there is a need for a Logistics Section, and, if yes, will designate an individual to fill the position of the Logistics Section Chief. If a Logistics Section is not established, the Incident Commander will perform all logistical functions.

A separate Logistics Section is established dependant on:

  • Size of the incident

  • Complexity of support needs

  • Incident length

Note

Note

It is important to remember that the Logistics Section is geared to supporting personnel and resources directly assigned to the incident.

For example, the Food Unit of the Logistics Section does not provide feeding services for people who have been sent to shelters during a flood.

Units in the Logistics Section are responsible for providing services and support to meet all incident or event needs. This is accomplished under the direction of the Logistics Section Chief. A deputy Logistics Section Chief may be assigned as needed.

Logistics services and support to an incident or event are important functions. Early recognition of the need for a Logistics function or section can reduce the amount of time and money spent on an incident.

The Logistics Section Chief has responsibility for six principal units or sets of activities at an incident.

The Logistics Section Chief may establish separate Branches for one or more of the logistics support or service activities (this will be explained later).

Logistics Section Chief

  • Provides resources and services

  • Develops Logistics portions of Incident Action Planand forward it to the Planning Section

  • Contracts for and purchases goods and services

It is up to the Logistics Section Chief to activate any additional staffing necessary to complete the tasks.

The Logistics Section can be staffed by two Branches (the Service Branch and the Support Branch) and six Units (the Communications Unit, Medical Unit, Food Unit, Supply Unit, Facilities Unit and the Ground Support Unit).

Note : Not all the units may be required; they will be established based on need. The titles of the units are descriptive of their responsibilities.

Service Branch

  • Established if Span of Control is an issue (Logistics Section).

  • Provides direct services to responders

  • Can be staffed to include a Communications Unit, Medical Unit and Food Unit

Communications Unit

  • Incident Communication Plan (ICS Form 205)

  • Distributes and maintains communications equipment

  • Manages Incident Communications Centre

  • Establishes adequate communications for the incident

Note : The Communications Unit in the Logistics Section deals with the logistical aspects of communications for the responders. It should not be confused with media relations and overall incident messaging which is the responsibility of the Information Officer.

Medical Unit

  • Develops Medical Plan (ICS Form 206)

  • Provides first aid and light medical treatment

  • Prepares procedures for a major medical emergency

  • Prepares any medical reports

Note : The Medical Unit is intended for the responders. If the incident itself involves medical treatment for the public then that falls under the Operations section, perhaps as a Medical Branch or Medical Group.

Food Unit

  • Determining and supplying the feeding and potable water requirements for incident personnel at all incident facilities, and for active resources within the Operations Section

  • Obtains equipment and supplies to operate food service facilities at Bases and Camps

The Food Unit may prepare menus and food, provide them through catering services, or use some combination of both methods. In some communities, Non-Governmental Organizations’s, or NGOs, such as the Salvation Army provide food and water to responders at large incidents.

Note : As with the Medical Unit, the Food Unit supplies food and water for the responders. If feeding of the public is required that, again, falls under the Operations Section.

Support Branch

In addition to the Service Branch, there also may be a Support Branch in the Logistics Section. The Support Branch is “hardware” oriented.

This branch may include a Supply Unit, Facilities Unit and Ground Support Unit.

Supply Unit

  • Determines type and amount of supplies needed to support the incident

  • Orders personnel, equipment and supplies

  • Receives, stores, distributes and maintains supplies

  • Services non-expendable equipment

  • Maintains inventory and accountability of supplies and equipment

  • Places all resource orders

If the Supply Unit has not been established, the responsibility for ordering rests with the Logistics Section Chief.

Facilities Unit

  • Sets up and maintains facilities may be required in support of the incident

  • Provides Managers for Base and Camps

  • Provides facility security and maintenance services such as sanitation, lighting, and cleanup as required

Note : If facilities are required for victims of the incident, that responsibility would lie within Operations, or more likely by the ECC at an off-site location.

Ground Support Unit

  • Prepares Transportation Plan

  • Arranges for, activates, and documents the fuelling and maintenance of assigned ground resources

  • Arranges for transportation of personnel, supplies, food and equipment
