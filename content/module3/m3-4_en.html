Establishment and Transfer of Command


2. Establishment and Transfer of Command

Command:

The act of directing, ordering or controlling by virtue of explicit statutory, regulatory or delegated authority.

Command at an incident is initially established by the highest ranking individual from the authority that first arrives at the scene, and has jurisdiction for the incident. The Incident Commander should have the level of training, experience and expertise to serve in this capacity. It is possible that the Incident Commander may not be the highest ranking officer on scene.

The Incident Commander has the authority to assume command.

The process of moving responsibility for incident command from one Incident Commander to another is called transfer of command.

    • there is a normal turnover of personnel. For example, to accommodate work/rest requests.
    • the incident response is concluded and responsibility is transferred to the home agency.
