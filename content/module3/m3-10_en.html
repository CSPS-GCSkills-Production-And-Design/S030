Manageable Span of Control

 

8. Manageable Span of Control

  • Number of individuals or resources that one person can manage effectively during an incident. Maintaining an effective span of control is particularly important on incidents where safety and accountability are a top priority.

  • Key to effective and efficient incident management. Supervisors must be able to adequately supervise and control their subordinates, as well as communicate with and manage all resources under their supervision.

 

Span of Control considerations areinfluenced by:

  • Type of incident, which will determine how many, what kind and what type of resources will be needed

  • Nature of the task and whether or not specialized resources will be needed

  • Hazards and safety factors which will drive the need for more or less resources must be considered

  • Distances between personnel and resources

 

The ICS span of control for any supervisor:

  • Is between three and seven subordinates

  • Optimally does not exceed five subordinates

If the number of reporting elements falls outside these ranges, expansion or consolidation of the organization may be necessary. There may be exceptions, usually in lower risk assignments or where resources work in close proximity to each other.

Some resources, such as a 20 person forest fire crew or a structural fire engine staffed with a 4 person crew, are considered single resources.
