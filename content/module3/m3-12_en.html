10. Incident Locations & Facilities

Various types of operational locations and support facilities are established in the vicinity of an incident to accomplish a variety of purposes, such as decontamination, the processing of donated goods, mass care, and evacuation. The Incident Commander establishes these facilities based on the requirements and complexity of the incident. Facilities may include the Incident Command Post, Base, Staging Area, Camp, Helibase and Helispot. Please click on each for more detailed information.

Note: Unit 6 will cover ICS facilities in more detail.

Incident Command Post

The Incident Command Post, or ICP, is the field location at which the primary tactical-level, on scene incident command functions are performed.

The ICP may be co-located with the Incident Base or other incident facilities, and is normally identified by a green rotating or flashing light.

Staging Area

A Staging Area is a location established where available resources can be placed while awaiting a tactical assignment.

Helibase

The Helibase is the location from which helicopter-centered air operations are conducted.

Base

The Base is the location at which primary Logistics and administrative functions for an incident are coordinated and administered. There is only one Base per incident. The incident name or another designator will be added to the term Base. The Incident Command Post may be co-located with the Base.

Camp

A Camp is a facility within the general incident area, separate from the Incident Base, equipped and staffed to provide sleeping, food, water, and sanitary services to incident personnel.
This can include hotels.

Helispot

Helispots are more temporary locations at the incident, where helicopters can safely land and take off.
