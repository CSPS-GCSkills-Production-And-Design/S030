Accountability

 

13. Accountability

Effective personnel accountability during incident operations is essential at all jurisdictional levels and within functional areas. Individuals must abide by their agency policies and guidelines and any applicable local, provincial or federal rules, regulations, bylaws and legislation.

Features and activities that contribute to personnel accountability:

Check-in

  • Check-In: All responders, regardless of agency affiliation, must report in to receive an assignment in accordance with the procedures established by the Incident Commander. Check-in officially logs a responder in at the incident. The check-in process and information helps to:

  • Ensure personnel accountability

  • Track resources

  • Prepare personnel for assignments and reassignments

  • Locate personnel in case of emergency

  • Establish personnel time records and payroll documentation

  • Plan for releasing personnel, and

  • Organize the demobilization process

Incident Action Plan

  • Incident Action Plan: Response operations must be directed and coordinated as outlined in the IAP. An assignment list in the IAP identifies resources with active assignments in the Operations Section.

Unity of Command

  • Unity of Command: Each individual involved in incident operations will be assigned to only one supervisor.

Span of Control

  • Span of Control: Supervisors must be able to adequately supervise and control their subordinates, as well as communicate with and manage all resources under their supervision.

Resource Tracking

  • Resource Tracking: Supervisors must record and report resource status changes as they occur.

Resources Unit

  • Resources Unit: Maintains the status of all assigned resources.
